I am a Ph.D. student in the Computer Science department at the University of Maryland, College Park, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. John Dickerson and Prof. Aravind Srinivasan .My research interests broadly focuses two directions : (1) Algorithms for online stochastic matching models I am interested in design and competitive analysis of algorithms under different online stochastic settings like KIID (Known IID distribution arrivals) and Random Order Arrivals. (2) Algorithmic fairness in combinatorial optimization. I am interested in understanding the algorithmic challenges of incorporating "acceptable" and "meaningful" notions of fairness into stochastic and combinatorial optimization problems arising in ride-sharing, internet advertising, crowdsourcing markets, kidney-exchange markets, etc. Further, I am interested in understanding the merits and limitations of the fairness criteria in providing solutions to the underlying optimization problem. My current research therefore spans Randomized Algorithms, Probabilistic Models and Fairness.

Before that, I obtained my Masters in Computer Science from Stony Brook University where I was advised by Prof. Rezaul Chowdhury. My thesis was focussed on reusable resource-allocation prolem.
